How to use this 💡

Two things to know and you're done: paste a link, and look at the monthly number. Everything else is optional.

The green 📋 Paste listing button floats at the bottom-right of every screen — that's the only button you really need.

1. Found a house you like?

On Zillow / Realtor / RedfinTap Share → Copy Link
Open this appTap Paste from clipboard
ThenTap ❤️ Save to our list

That's it. It figures out the address from the link, looks up the county's records, and works out what it really costs. You don't type anything.

2. The number that matters

A listing says $400,000. That is not what you pay. What actually leaves your bank account every month is the mortgage plus property tax, home insurance, mortgage insurance, and HOA fees.

This app shows you that real number — True monthly — right at the top of every home. Two houses at the same price can be $300/month apart because of taxes alone. That's the whole reason this app exists.

Tap Cost detail on any home to also see cash to close — the money you need on day one, which is the down payment plus several thousand in closing costs nobody warns you about.

3. The Fit score

The big colored number. It's how well a house matches what we said we care about — schools, budget, commute, the neighborhood, all of it in one figure.

72+ Strong fitworth a showing
50–71 Mixedsomething's off — tap in and see what
under 50 Poor fitprobably not for us

You can change what it weighs: Money tab → Our priorities. Tap Her defaults and it leans on schools, safety, and a stable street. Nothing is permanent — slide things around and every house re-scores instantly.

4. The one weird Ohio thing

In Franklin County, the city on your mailing address is not always the school district you're in. There are houses with a Hilliard address that go to Dublin schools, and Columbus addresses that go to Hilliard schools.

Zillow will not tell you this. This app checks the county's own records on every home and puts a ⚠ MIXED DISTRICT stamp on it if the address and schools don't match. If you see that stamp, read what it says — it'll tell you exactly which schools you'd actually be in.

The whole process, start to finish

Nobody lays this out for first-time buyers. Here it is.

1
Get pre-approved (before looking seriously)Not "pre-qualified" — pre-approved. It's free, takes a day, and tells you the real number. Talk to 2–3 lenders on the same day; rates and fees genuinely differ, and a soft window means the credit pulls count as one.
2
Set the ceiling, then lookUse the affordability calculator on the Money tab. Never tour a house above it — you'll fall in love and rationalize.
3
Tour, and use the showing checklistEvery saved home has one. Come back at night before you offer.
4
Make an offerIncludes earnest money (1–2%, credited to you at closing) and contingencies. Keep the inspection, financing, and appraisal contingencies. Waiving them is how first-time buyers get destroyed.
5
Inspection (~7–10 days)$400–500, plus a $150–250 sewer scope and a radon test — both essential in Columbus. Whatever it finds is a renegotiation, not automatically a dealbreaker.
6
AppraisalThe bank checks the house is worth what you're paying. If it appraises low, you renegotiate, pay the gap in cash, or walk.
7
Underwriting (~30 days)Do not change jobs, open a credit card, or buy a car. People lose loans doing this at the finish line.
8
ClosingYou bring cash to close (down payment + closing costs). Final walkthrough the same day — make sure nothing broke and the seller took their junk.

Start to finish is typically 30–45 days from accepted offer.

Words people will use at you 📖

Escrow

Two meanings. (1) A neutral third party holding money during the sale. (2) The account your lender uses to collect property tax and insurance monthly, then pay them for you. That's why your payment is bigger than the mortgage alone.

PMI / MIP

Mortgage insurance. Protects the lender, not you. You pay it when you put less than 20% down. On a conventional loan it cancels automatically once you've got 22% equity. On FHA with low down payment it lasts the life of the loan — you escape it by refinancing.

Earnest money

A deposit (1–2%) proving you're serious. It's credited toward your down payment at closing. You get it back if you walk for a protected reason. You lose it if you just change your mind.

Contingency

An escape hatch written into your offer. "Inspection contingency" = I can walk if the inspection is bad, and keep my earnest money. Sellers prefer offers without them. Do not be the person who waives them.

Appraisal gap

When the bank's appraiser values the house below your offer. The bank only lends against the appraised value, so you must cover the difference in cash, renegotiate, or walk.

Closing costs

Fees to finalize the loan and transfer the house — origination, title, appraisal, recording. Roughly 2–4% of the price, on top of your down payment. Sellers can be asked to cover some.

Points

Prepaid interest. One point = 1% of the loan, buying a slightly lower rate. Worth it only if you'll stay long enough to break even — ask the lender for that number.

Under contract / pending / contingent

Someone's offer was accepted. "Contingent" means it could still fall through. Not worth chasing unless you want to be the backup.

Millage / effective tax rate

How Ohio calculates property tax. What matters to you is the effective rate — the app derives it from what neighbors actually pay.

Triennial reappraisal

Ohio counties re-value homes on a cycle. If you paid far above the county's current value, your tax bill will rise toward what you paid. The app flags this on every home — it's the most common nasty surprise for new owners.

If something looks broken

The neighborhood scans use a free public service that gets busy. If a layer says "failed," wait about 30 seconds and tap it again — it usually just works. Map tab → Data check will tell you exactly which piece is having a problem.

And everything here is an estimate for planning. The real numbers come from a lender and the county auditor. This app's job is to save you from researching thirty houses to find the three worth a real look.
